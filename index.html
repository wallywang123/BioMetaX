Two global challenges — one biotech solution

Powering metal recovery from waste using greenhouse gases and ultra-low-cost biotechnologies.

The Issue

Industries and the global community are racing toward Net-Zero emissions by 2050 under the Paris Agreement.

Meanwhile, electronic waste and spent batteries are piling up — 2.6 million tons each year — with only ~20% being recycled. Boosting the recycling rate to 60% could unlock around USD 38 billion in value.

Treating tailings while recovering metals creates a win-win solution for both the environment and industry.

Our Solution

We use naturally enriched functional microorganisms to recover metals (Gold, Nickel, and Cobalt) using waste gases (including CH4 and CO2) from landfills, syngases, or wastewater treatment plants.

This process simultaneously cuts greenhouse gas emissions and recovers valuable metals from electronic waste, spent batteries, or tailings.

Our Version

We are currently planning to scale up our laboratory-level reactors to pilot-scale and are seeking more resources and collaborations.

We are expanding applications in e-waste recycling, spent battery treatment, tailings management, and heavy metal-containing wastewater treatment. We also welcome discussions with sectors of landfill gas and wastewater treatment plants to explore innovative solutions together.
