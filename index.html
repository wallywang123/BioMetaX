The issue

Industries and the global community are racing toward Net-Zero emissions by 2050 under the Paris Agreement.

Meanwhile, electronic waste and spent batteries are piling up — 2.6 million tons each year — with only ~20% being recycled. Boosting the recycling rate to 60% could unlock around USD 38 billion in value.

At the same time, treating tailings while recovering metals creates a win-win solution for both the environment and industry.

Our solution

To address metallurgy, tailings, and landfills challenges, reduce carbon emissions, and cut metal recovery costs, we developed a novel biological solution.

We use naturally enriched functional microorganisms to recover metals (Gold, Nickel, and Cobalt etc.) using waste gases (including CH4 and CO2) from landfills, syngases, or wastewater treatment plants.

This process simultaneously cuts greenhouse gas emissions and recovers valuable metals from electronic waste, spent batteries, or tailings. We are also advancing carbon utilization platforms to move toward zero-carbon solutions.

We welcome global partnerships with e-waste recyclers, battery manufacturers, tailings operators, landfill gas suppliers, and wastewater treatment plants to drive innovation and scale our impact.

Our version

We are currently planning to scale up our laboratory-level reactors to pilot-scale and are seeking more resources and collaborations.

We are expanding the application scenarios of our technology in sectors such as e-waste recycling, spent battery treatment, metal catalyst development, tailings management, and heavy metal-containing wastewater treatment. We also welcome discussions and collaboration opportunities with landfill gas and wastewater treatment sectors to explore innovative solutions together.
